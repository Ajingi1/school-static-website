Electronic Department

Radio and Television (Electronic) Department

The Radio and Television (Electronic) department at Government Technical College (GTC) Tiga focuses on providing students with the knowledge and skills required to work in the fields of radio, television, and electronics. This department is designed to prepare students for technical roles in the electronics industry, emphasizing both theoretical knowledge and practical skills.

Key Aspects of the Department

Curriculum

  • Basic Electronics: Introduction to electronic components and circuits.
  • Radio Communication: Principles of radio wave transmission and reception.
  • Television Technology: Understanding of television systems, both analog and digital.
  • Electronic Devices and Circuits: Detailed study of electronic devices such as transistors, diodes, and integrated circuits.
  • Practical Skills: Hands-on experience with assembling, testing, and repairing electronic equipment.

Laboratory Work

The department is equipped with labs where students can practice what they learn in theory. Labs contain modern equipment for testing and troubleshooting radio and television sets, as well as other electronic devices.

Workshops

Workshops provide additional practical training. Students work on real-world projects, enhancing their problem-solving skills and technical knowledge.

Career Opportunities

Graduates can pursue careers as radio and television technicians, broadcast engineers, and electronic repair specialists. The department also provides a solid foundation for further studies in electronics and communication engineering.

Certifications

Students receive vocational and professional certifications upon completion of their program. These certifications are recognized by industry and can help graduates in securing employment.

Industry Collaboration

The department may collaborate with local industries for internships and job placements. Such collaborations ensure that the training is aligned with current industry standards and requirements.

Goals of the Department

  • Skill Development: Equip students with the necessary skills to excel in the electronics and communication industry.
  • Quality Education: Provide high-quality education through a combination of theoretical and practical learning.
  • Employment Readiness: Prepare students for immediate employment in various technical roles related to radio, television, and electronics.
  • Further Education: Enable students to pursue higher education in related fields.

The Radio and Television (Electronic) department at GTC Tiga is committed to producing competent technicians who can meet the demands of the rapidly evolving electronics industry.

Mercanical Department

Mechanical Department

The Mechanical Department at Government Technical College (GTC) Tiga provides students with the skills and knowledge required for careers in mechanical engineering and related fields. This department offers comprehensive training in various aspects of mechanical engineering, including design, maintenance, and operation of mechanical systems.

Key Aspects of the Department

Curriculum

  • Engineering Fundamentals: Introduction to basic principles of engineering, including mechanics, thermodynamics, and materials science.
  • Mechanical Design: Training in designing and analyzing mechanical components and systems using computer-aided design (CAD) software.
  • Manufacturing Processes: Study of various manufacturing techniques such as machining, casting, and welding.
  • Automotive Technology: Understanding vehicle systems, including engines, transmissions, and suspension systems.
  • Robotics and Automation: Introduction to robotics principles and programming for automated systems.
  • Safety Practices: Emphasis on safety procedures and regulations in mechanical engineering.

Laboratory Work

The department is equipped with modern laboratories where students can apply theoretical knowledge. These labs provide hands-on experience in mechanical design, manufacturing, and testing.

Workshops

Workshops offer practical training opportunities where students work on real-world mechanical projects, enhancing their skills and problem-solving abilities.

Career Opportunities

Graduates can pursue careers in various fields such as mechanical design, manufacturing, automotive engineering, and robotics. The department also prepares students for further studies in mechanical engineering and related disciplines.

Certifications

Students receive vocational and professional certifications upon completing their program, which are recognized by industry and can enhance employment prospects.

Industry Collaboration

The department collaborates with local industries and businesses for internships and job placements, ensuring that the training is aligned with industry standards and requirements.

Goals of the Department

  • Skills Development: Equip students with the skills and knowledge required to succeed in the field of mechanical engineering.
  • Quality Education: Provide high-quality education through a blend of theoretical learning and practical experience.
  • Employment Readiness: Prepare students for immediate employment in various technical roles within the mechanical engineering industry.
  • Continued Education: Enable students to pursue further education and specialization in mechanical engineering or related fields.

The Mechanical Department at GTC Tiga is committed to producing skilled professionals who can meet the demands of the mechanical engineering industry.
